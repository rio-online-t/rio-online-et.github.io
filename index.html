Radar-Inertial Odometry with Online Spatio-Temporal Calibration via Continuous-Time IMU Modeling

Vlaho-Josip Štironja, Luka Petrović, Juraj Peršić, Ivan Marković, Ivan Petrović
University of Zagreb, Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computing
Laboratory for Autonomous Systems and Mobile Robotics (LAMOR)
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

Radar-Inertial Odometry (RIO) has emerged as a robust alternative to vision-based and lidar-based odometry in challenging conditions such as low light, fog, featureless environments, or in adverse weather. However, many existing RIO approaches assume known radar-IMU extrinsic calibration or rely on sufficient motion excitation for online extrinsic estimation, while temporal misalignment between sensors is often neglected or treated independently. In this work, we present a RIO framework that performs joint online spatial and temporal calibration within a factor-graph optimization formulation, based on continuous-time modeling of inertial measurements using uniform cubic B-splines. The proposed continuous-time representation of acceleration and angular velocity accurately captures the asynchronous nature of radar-IMU measurements, enabling reliable convergence of both temporal offset and extrinsic calibration parameters, without relying on scan matching, target tracking, or environment-specific assumptions.

Methodology

Factor graph architecture: IMU factor (fIMU), radar ego-velocity factor (fR), constant time-offset factor (fCT), and constant extrinsic calibration factor (fCE).

The proposed method, dubbed LC-RIO-ET (Loosely Coupled Radar-Inertial Odometry with Extrinsic and Temporal Calibration), performs joint online spatial and temporal calibration formulated within a factor graph optimization (FGO) framework. The key contribution is the use of uniform cubic B-splines to model inertial signals in continuous time, enabling acceleration and angular velocity estimation at arbitrary timestamps while maintaining C²-continuity. Given discrete IMU measurements within a window centered on the radar timestamp, independent B-splines are fit to the raw acceleration and angular velocity signals. This continuous-time inertial model is directly embedded into the radar ego-velocity factor, allowing the temporal offset and extrinsic calibration parameters to be co-estimated within the same optimization. The system is implemented with GTSAM using iSAM2 within a one-second sliding window, with a two-thread architecture separating optimization and real-time navigation.

Results


Temporal Offset Convergence


(a) Temporal offset convergence of LC-RIO-ET under various initial values (0, 25, 50, 75, 100, 125 ms). In all cases the estimate converges robustly to a consistent steady-state of ~113.3 ms.

(b) Comparison of temporal offset trajectories for LC-RIO-ET, EKF-RIO-TC, and RIO-T on Sequence 5. All methods converge to a consistent offset, validating the B-spline approach.

LC-RIO-ET converges reliably to the temporal offset from all six initializations, demonstrating that the B-spline-based radar factor provides a well-conditioned gradient signal over a wide initialization range. Unlike RIO-T, which relies on locally constant acceleration and requires multiple iterations to resolve large offsets, LC-RIO-ET achieves convergence online even for larger temporal offsets thanks to its continuous-time inertial model.


Odometry Accuracy – EKF-RIO-TC Dataset (Unsynchronized)


LC-RIO-ET achieves the best mean performance across all four metrics on the unsynchronized EKF-RIO-TC dataset, with a mean translational RPE of 0.138 m and rotational RPE of 2.377°, representing improvements of 18.3% and 4.5% respectively over EKF-RIO-TC. Relative to the plain FGO baseline LC-RIO, the translational RPE improves by 33.0% and the translational APE by 43.9%, confirming that jointly estimating both calibration parameters is substantially more beneficial than either alone.

Odometry Accuracy – ICINS Dataset (Hardware-Synchronized)


On the hardware-synchronized ICINS dataset, LC-RIO-ET performs on par with LC-RIO, with nearly identical mean translational RPE (0.151 m vs. 0.150 m), confirming that the method does not degrade performance in synchronized settings. Notably, LC-RIO-ET achieves the lowest mean rotational APE (4.864°), suggesting a residual benefit of the continuous-time inertial model even in synchronized conditions. The estimated offset on this dataset converges to approximately −10 ms, consistent with the hardware trigger ensuring a small temporal offset.

Impact of Online Spatio-Temporal Calibration


Trajectory visualization of EKF-RIO with original (blue) and LC-RIO-ET estimated (green) parameters on Sequence 5 from the EKF-RIO-TC dataset.


To isolate the value of calibrated parameters, EKF-RIO and LC-RIO were re-initialized with extrinsic and temporal offset parameters estimated by LC-RIO-ET (denoted *), and re-run with these fixed calibration values. On Sequence 5 (unsynchronized, high dynamics), calibrated parameters reduce translational RPE of EKF-RIO by 68.4% and of LC-RIO by 51.2%. On the flight 2 sequence, improvements are smaller but still consistent, reducing translational RPE by 6.3% for EKF-RIO* and 1.9% for LC-RIO* while also improving rotational metrics.
